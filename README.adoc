= LVM Expansion Troubleshooting Lab

This lab teaches systematic troubleshooting and LVM expansion through a real-world scenario where a filesystem has run out of space but has additional capacity available on the underlying disk.

== Lab Overview

Students will work through a disk space emergency at a fictitious company (Super-Business) where a manager created a filesystem that's too small. The lab follows a methodical troubleshooting approach: diagnosis, analysis, and resolution.

=== Learning Objectives

* Diagnose disk space issues using `df`, `du`, and LVM commands
* Understand LVM architecture (physical volumes, volume groups, logical volumes)
* Safely extend logical volumes and grow filesystems
* Follow systematic troubleshooting methodology
* Differentiate between logical volume extension and filesystem growth

=== Lab Structure

The lab consists of four modules:

. **Introduction** - Presents the scenario through emails from "Manager Scott" and guidance from "Sysadmin Nate"
. **Diagnosis** - Students investigate the current state using diagnostic commands
. **Analysis** - Students plan the expansion by understanding LVM structure and available resources
. **Resolution** - Students execute the LVM and filesystem expansion

=== Technical Details

**Infrastructure:**

* 1 RHEL 10 VM with 4GB RAM
* Primary disk (40GB) for OS
* Secondary disk (5GB) for application data

**Initial State:**

* Secondary disk (`/dev/vdb`) configured with LVM
* Volume group `app_vg` created on full 5GB disk
* Logical volume `app_lv` sized at only 1GB
* XFS filesystem mounted at `/app`
* Filesystem filled to ~96% capacity
* ~4GB of free space available in volume group

**Solution:**

Students will extend the logical volume to use all available VG space and grow the XFS filesystem to match.

== Key Concepts Covered

* **Filesystem vs Logical Volume**: Understanding the difference and why both must be resized
* **LVM Components**: Physical volumes (PV), volume groups (VG), and logical volumes (LV)
* **Diagnostic Tools**: `df`, `du`, `pvdisplay`, `vgdisplay`, `lvdisplay`
* **LVM Commands**: `lvextend` with percentage-based sizing
* **Filesystem Growth**: `xfs_growfs` for online XFS expansion
* **Troubleshooting Methodology**: Systematic approach to problem-solving

== Pedagogical Approach

The lab uses fictional personas to add engagement and realism:

* **Manager Scott**: A well-meaning but technically rusty manager who creates the problem
* **Sysadmin Nate**: An experienced mentor who provides helpful guidance

This approach makes the scenario more engaging while teaching both technical skills and professional communication.
